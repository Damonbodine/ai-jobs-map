The recommendation engine now runs across all occupations by default.

This document defines the next override priorities so calibration stays focused on the occupations that matter most commercially and narratively.

The rule is:

- generated by default across the full inventory
- curated overrides for roles with high traffic, high commercial value, or unusually specific operational context

## Selection Heuristics

Add a curated override when at least one of these is true:

1. the role has meaningful commercial relevance
2. the generated language is technically correct but too generic
3. the role has high domain sensitivity
4. the support story is easy to tell and worth tightening
5. the role is likely to be shown in demos, sales, or investor conversations

## QA Process

### Weekly Calibration Pass

Review 10 to 20 roles at a time:

- 5 from Tier 1
- 3 from Tier 2
- 2 to 5 from Tier 3 or Tier 4

For each role, check:

- does the primary support recommendation make sense?
- does the secondary recommendation feel plausible?
- do the `What it handles` bullets sound like real work?
- does `What stays with you` feel trust-building rather than generic?
- does the package bridge feel aligned with the support recommendation?

### Override Decision

If the generated output is:

- good enough: leave it generated
- close but generic: improve role-profile calibration
- clearly off: add a curated override

## Guiding Principle

Do not try to hand-author the entire catalog.

The point of overrides is not to replace the engine.
The point is to sharpen the engine in the places where:

- the role matters most
- the story can be especially compelling
- the generated output still undershoots what the role deserves
